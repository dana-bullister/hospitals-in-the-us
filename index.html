Hospitals in the United States, 2020

The spread of COVID-19 has shed light on the alarming lack of access to hospitals for those living in more sparsely populated regions of the country.

While those in urban and eastern regions often have multiple options for facilities, those in rural or western areas frequently must travel hundreds of miles to obtain specialized care.

This reality highlights the need for better infrastructure for telemedicine, which isn't covered by many insurers, and reassessment of our distribution of hospital services.

Number of US Hospitals Over Time

Lack of convenient, proximal access to hospitals is exacerbated by a decades long trend of steady hospital closures. Reasons for this include hospital mergers and acquisitions resulting in consolidation of services; more efficient, rapid care; more care that is shifted to outpatient settings; and decisions by some states not to expand Medicaid as part of the Affordable Care Act.

Regardless, closures have resulted in increased time and distance to accessing critical services, which for many conditions can lead to worse outcomes (learn more here). In the face of current unprecedented health challenges, it is imperative that we investigate and strategize deliberately regarding the effective provision of critical, life-saving services.
